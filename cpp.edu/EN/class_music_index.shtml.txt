<p><span>As we are emerging fully from the COVID-19 pandemic experience, I am noticing that we have learned a few lessons.</span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span>First and foremost, music is a crucial aspect of human existence. We need music to come together, we need music to feel better, and we need music to heal. We at Cal Poly Pomona were able to emerge from COVID-19 with renewed vigor and enthusiasm because I think we all realize this centrality of music on our lives. Our ensembles are performing with renewed vigor, our recording studios are busy, our hallways and practice rooms active again.</span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span>I think we have also realized that much can be accomplished online. If designed correctly, some classes that were converted to being totally or partially online could still support student learning as well as in-person classes. Meetings can be successful online, and some lessons and master classes, too. We can have copyright experts from New York and Nashville &#8220;drop in&#8221; on our classes though Zoom or Skype, just as we can access archives of music and research at the Library of Congress that are swiftly being digitally converted. We don&#8217;t have to print and sign all documents and wait for someone to follow them along a paper trail, we now have a digital trail. These are all innovations that have accelerated and lingered in the last three years.</span></p>
</div>
<div>

Hanging out is essential to making music, too, and we are celebrating the return of socializing in the hallways and at &#8220;</span><a href="https://www.cpp.edu/class/music/about-department/couches" rel="noopener" target="_blank">the couches</a><span>.&#8221;</span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span>If you are contemplating applying to Cal Poly Pomona as a musician, or even just interested in music as a hobby, come on by!</span></p>
<p><span>Contact me to book a tour of the facilities with your family, drop in on a songwriting or conducting class</span><span>, come to a concert</span><span>, have a look at our recording studios, or come to meeting of our student clubs</span><span>. By all means audition for one of ouf many ensembles (they</span><span>&#160;are&#160;</span><span>all</span><span>&#160;open to non-majors&#8211; just contact the ensemble director &#8211; and we have instruments to rent at</span><span>&#160;a ridiculously low price).</span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span>You will likely come to the same conclusion we have over the last three years:&#160;<em><strong>You Need This!</strong></em></span></p>

                        <div class="modal-body" tabindex="0">
                            <p>Founded in 1963, the Cal Poly Pomona Music Department has grown into a leading institution in artistic education. Accredited by the National Association of Schools of Music (NASM), our department offers a comprehensive curriculum guided by our esteemed faculty members, serving over 200 majors who share a passion for music and commitment to excellence.</p>
<p>Our diverse undergraduate programs, including a <em><strong>Bachelors of Arts in Music</strong></em>, <em><strong>Bachelor of Music in Performance, Composition, Music Ed/Pedagogy</strong>,</em> and the pioneering <em><strong>Bachelor of Arts in Music Industry Studies</strong></em>, offer you specialized paths to cultivate your talents. Engage in ensembles such as the <em><strong>University Concert Band</strong></em>, <em><strong>Symphony Orchestra</strong></em>, and <em><strong>Jazz Band</strong></em> to experience the collaborative spirit of Cal Poly Pomona's <em><strong>"Learn by Doing"</strong></em> philosophy. This nurturing environment, enriched by faculty mentorship and real-world job experiences, prepares you for dynamic careers in the music industry.</p>
<p>Community engagement is a cornerstone of our mission, with nearly 70 annual events including faculty concerts, guest artist performances, and ensemble showcases. Performances by the acclaimed Mariachi ensemble, <em><strong>Mariachi Los Broncos y Caballeros de Cal Poly Pomona</strong></em>, unite the campus and surrounding areas in a shared appreciation for music. Committed to integrating modern technology and industry practices, the Music Department fosters the growth of passionate musicians and industry leaders, inspiring resilience and continual improvement in a vibrant, supportive community.</p>
<p>Every semester the highly anticipated&#160;<em><strong>Songwriter Showcase</strong></em> celebrates the creativity and talent of our student songwriters. This dynamic showcase provides a platform for students to perform their original compositions in front of an enthusiastic audience, including fellow students, faculty, and the broader community.</p>
