            menu.removeClass("hidden");
            menu.addClass("show");

<li>Student converts loans to Work Study or Work Study (unearned) to loans</li>
<li>Student is no longer enrolled</li>
</ul></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="Starting and Ending FWS Employment" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">Starting and Ending FWS Employment</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p>A student may begin earning Work Study on the first day of classes, provided all paperwork has been completed.&#160;</p>
<ul>
<li>For an <strong>academic year offer</strong>, a student may work from September 1 through the last day of finals in the spring semester in May.</li>
<li>For a <strong>fall only offer</strong>, a student may work from September 1 through the last day of finals for the fall semester.</li>
<li>For a <strong>spring only offer</strong>, a student may work from January 1 through the last day of finals for the spring term.</li>
<li><strong>Summer Work Study</strong> is not available.</li>
</ul>
<p>A student may earn up to their Work Study offered amount during the academic year, half each semester. Students may work up to 20 hours per week when classes are in session and up to 40 hours per week when classes are not in session (i.e., Spring Break).</p>
<h2><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Conditions of Employment for students:</strong></span></h2>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><strong>A student must stop working immediately if any of the following conditions apply to them:</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li style="list-style-type: none;">
<ul>
<li>When they reach their Earnings Limit</li>
<li>If they have a financial aid hold (check BroncoDirect)</li>
<li>If they drop below half-time enrollment at any time during the semester</li>
<li>If they are no longer eligible for aid under the Cal Poly Pomona Satisfactory Academic Progress Standards for aid applicants.</li>
<li>If they are graduating, they must stop working on the last day of finals for the semester</li>
<li>If they are not graduating, they must stop working on the last day of finals for the semester (<strong>not the last day of the pay period</strong>).</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Unearned Fall Semester amounts</strong></span></h2>
<p>If a student does not earn up to their full Earnings Limit during the fall semester, they can use unearned amounts in the spring &#8212; but they cannot be automatically carried over to the summer period.</p></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="Billing of Department Budgets" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">Billing of Department Budgets</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">

                                        <p class="body2"><p><strong>Note: ASI/Foundation employers will be billed the 25%.</strong></p>
<h2><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>ASI-Foundation Accounts</strong></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>You are responsible for 25% of the student's total pay each month.</li>
<li>University Financial Service's Billing Department will send you a monthly invoice.</li>
<li>Payment will be due upon receipt of the invoice.</li>
<li>For questions or assistance regarding billing, please contact Jessica Lopez, Accounts Specialist at 909-869-2024.</li>
</ul></p>

                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="Your Responsibilities as a FWS Employer" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">Your Responsibilities as a FWS Employer</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><ul>
<li>To clearly define employment expectations and standards.</li>
<li>Keep a copy of the Job Listing you submitted on Handshake and review with your Federal Work Study students when they begin work.</li>
<li>To ensure that a Federal Work Study student does not begin working until all employment documents have been submitted to Human Resources and the student receives a clearance form from Human Resources.</li>
<li>To respond to all requests from the Office of Financial Aid and Scholarships in a timely manner.</li>
<li>To sign all <a href="../forms/fws-timesheet.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Timesheets (PDF)</a> and Student Assistant Attendance Reports and submit paperwork by the date and time due each month. Remember, you are verifying the student worked the hours recorded when you sign the Student Assistant Time Report and/or timesheets for Foundation employees.</li>
<li>To monitor the student&#8217;s earnings against their allocation each month to make sure they do not exceed the maximum for any semester. We have supplied an <a href="../forms/fws_award_balance-semester.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank">FWS Balance (PDF)</a> form that you can download and use for this purpose.</li>
<li>To pay for 100% of any earnings you allow a student to work in excess of the semester's Federal Work Study allocation.</li>
<li>To ensure no student is denied work or subjected to treatment under this program agreement on the grounds of race, color, national origin or gender.</li>
</ul></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="Maintain Compliance of Federal Work Study Employment" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">Maintain Compliance of Federal Work Study Employment</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><div><strong>In accordance with Federal Regulations, employment under the Work Study Program must follow the specific guidelines and conditions as outlined below.</strong></div>
<div></div>
<ol style="list-style-type: upper-alpha;">
<li>Undergraduate students must be paid hourly wages; however, graduate students can be paid hourly or on a salary.</li>
<li>Work must be regulated by employment conditions, including wages that are appropriate for the type of work, the geographic location, and proficiency of the employee.</li>
<li>Students must be paid at least the federal, state, or local minimum wage, whichever is higher.</li>
<li>Work Study employment must not displace employees and must not impair any existing service contracts. This includes filling jobs that are vacant because employees are on strike. Replacement is interpreted as displacement- You cannot replace a full-time employee, whose position was eliminated, with a student who is paid Work Study funds.</li>
<li>The Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938, as amended, prohibits employers (including schools) from accepting voluntary services from any paid employee. Any student employed under the Work Study Program must be paid for all hours worked. Volunteer work is not permitted. Work without pay is in direct violation of the conditions of Work Study employment.</li>
<li>Students are not permitted to work during scheduled class times. Exceptions include: When a class is cancelled:
<ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<li>An Instructor has excused the student from attending during a particular day</li>
<li>The student is receiving credit for their employment in an internship or externship</li>
<li>Any exceptions must be documented</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>A student may earn academic credit as well as compensation for Work Study jobs. However, a student employed in a Work Study job and receiving academic credit for that job may not be paid less than he or she would be if no academic credit were given.</li>
<li>Work Study does not subsidize fringe benefits such as sick leave, vacation pay, holiday pay, or employer&#8217;s contributions to Social Security, workers&#8217; compensation, retirement, any kind of leave or any other welfare or insurance program. Please note that students may be eligible for these types of benefits; however, they are disallowed benefits under the Work Study Program. If such costs are incurred, the department covers 100% of the cost.</li>
<li>You must maintain timesheet records of all hours worked by student workers. Timesheets must:
<ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<li>Be certified by the supervisor/ school official</li>
<li>Show the hours a student worked during each day and the total worked during each pay cycle</li>
<li>Match the hours for which the student is paid</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Solicitation or acceptance of gifts, fees, or commissions as a condition for a student&#8217;s work-study employment is prohibited.</li>
</ol>
<div></div></p>
