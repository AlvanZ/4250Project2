                                        <p class="body2"><p>A student&#8217;s FAFSA is considered complete and submitted on the date that <strong>all contributors</strong> (student, spouse, parent as applicable) complete and submit their sections.&#160; The date that all contributors have completed and submitted their sections is the official Application Receipt Date; this is true even if there is a delay between when the student started the application and when all contributors have completed their sections.</p>
<p>This date is important as the Application Receipt Date is the date used by the Student Aid Commission to determine a student&#8217;s eligibility for California State Grants and the date Cal Poly Pomona uses to determine eligibility for State University Grant.&#160; For 2024-2025, if a student's FAFSA is received by the FAFSA Processing System by April 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2024, the student is in the pool of students considered for Cal Grant, Middle Class Scholarship, and State University Grant funds.</p>
<h3><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><strong>Scenario 1:</strong></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The student submits their section on 1/15 and sends the invite to the parent contributor.</li>
<li>The parent submits their section on 2/15, so the FAFSA is complete.</li>
<li>The FAFSA is considered to be <strong>complete and submitted as of 2/15</strong> (Application Receipt Date is 2/15).</li>

<li>The student starts the FAFSA process over, submits their section on 5/15, and sends the invite to the parent contributor.</li>
<li>The parent submits their part on 5/18, so the FAFSA is complete.</li>
<li>The FAFA is considered to be <strong>complete and submitted as of 5/18</strong> (Application Receipt Date is 5/18).</li>

                                        <p class="body2"><p>Yes. In its <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kSHQmtSQFvo">FAFSA Changes: An Overview webinar</a>, the U.S. Department of Education (ED) stated, beginning with the 2024-25 FAFSA, all students and FAFSA contributors <strong>must </strong>create a <strong><em>Federal Student Aid ID</em></strong> (FSA username and password).</p>
<p>Users will no longer be allowed to access, create, or submit an online FAFSA using just their personally identifiable information (PII). They must be authenticated using an FSA ID. &#160;ED has confirmed all contributors to the FAFSA, including those without a social security number (SSN), will be able to create an FSA ID starting with the 2024-25 FAFSA cycle.</p>
<ul>
<li>If the contributor has an SSN, that SSN will be used to create the FSA ID.</li>
<li>For a contributor&#160;without an SSN, there will be a knowledge-based match to validate/authenticate their identity. Guidance and instructions for using the updated FSA ID process will be forthcoming (timeframe unspecified).</li>
<li>Foreign tax filers will continue to self-report their tax information on the FAFSA. They will also be able to create an FSA ID to access the online FAFSA even if they don't have an SSN.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Student Eligibility: </strong>Under <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subc66832d">34 CFR 668.32(d)</a> and <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subc66833">668.33</a>, a student must still be a U.S. citizen or eligible noncitizen to be eligible for federal student aid. Also, under <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subc66832i">668.32(i)</a>, except for students from the Freely Associated States, all students must have an SSN to be eligible for Title IV aid. Students from the Freely Associated States (Marshall Islands, Micronesia, and Palau) are assigned pseudo-SSNs.</p>
<p><strong>Paper FAFSA: </strong>The student or contributor does not need an FSA ID to file the paper FAFSA.&#160; The student applies using the paper FAFSA and obtains wet signatures from <strong>all contributors</strong>, including the parents who also affirm their consent.</p>
<p>Review this <a href="https://cpp.financialaidtv.com/play/1766-applying-financial-aid/38872-fafsa-creating-fsa-id" rel="noopener" target="_blank">video</a> for help in creating an FSA ID now (for those with an SSN).</p></p>

                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p>No. If a course is not required for completion of the student's academic program, it is considered an ineligible course. Federal (and State) law prohibit schools from including an ineligible course in a student's enrollment status or cost of attendance (COA), and the school cannot award federal or state funds to pay for the course.</p>
<p>According to guidance under <strong><em>Restriction on coursework/learning which is not Title IV-eligible</em></strong> in Volume 1 of&#160;the <em>Federal Student Aid (FSA) Handbook</em>, unless a course is a remedial course, it cannot be used to determine enrollment status if it does not count toward a degree, certificate, or recognized credential. Therefore, the student cannot receive Title IV aid for those courses.&#160;<strong>This also means that a student who completes the academic requirements for a program but does not yet have the degree or certificate may not receive additional Title IV funds for that program.</strong></p>
<p>To be eligible for federal (and state) funds, a student must be a regular student as defined in&#160;<a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_600_Institutional_Eligibility#suba6002">34 CFR 600.2</a>&#160;of the Institutional Eligibility regulations. A regular student is defined as: &#8220;A person who is enrolled or accepted for enrollment at an institution for the purpose of obtaining a degree, certificate, or other recognized educational credential offered by that institution.&#8221; Therefore, the school cannot award federal or state aid for course work that will not count towards the completion of that student's degree program requirements (or otherwise result in a degree, certificate, or credential).</p>
<p>Many degree programs include <strong><em>elective courses</em></strong> and, as such, students may receive federal and state aid only for those elective courses which are a required part of the program completion requirements. If audited or <em>pass/fail</em> courses are elective courses and the credits received will count towards program completion, they may be included in the student&#8217;s enrollment status. The same would be true for graduate level courses taken while enrolled in an undergraduate program; the student can only be awarded Title IV aid to cover the graduate courses if one or more of those graduate courses counted as elective credit towards the student's undergraduate degree program requirements.</p>
<p><strong>This restriction is not new.</strong></p></p>

                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="October 2022: Will the new California Ban on Private Scholarship Displacement (AB 288) change how finanical aid is processed at Cal Poly Pomona?" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">October 2022: Will the new California Ban on Private Scholarship Displacement (AB 288) change how finanical aid is processed at Cal Poly Pomona?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><strong>Answer:&#160; No.&#160; </strong>The new California law will not require any changes to how financial aid is processed at Cal Poly Pomona.</p>
<p><strong>Private </strong><strong>Scholarship Displacement</strong> is a practice where colleges and universities displace previously awarded financial aid when a student is awarded a <strong>private scholarship</strong>, effectively reducing the amount of financial aid awarded and resulting in a zero net gain for the student.</p>
<p>At <strong>Cal Poly Pomona</strong>, we believe that private scholarships should supplement, not supplant grants, tuition waivers or stipends provided by institutions of higher education to students who have a proven financial need. Limiting a student&#8217;s ability to pay for college can have significant consequences, including postponing graduation or dropping out of school, and runs counter to making a college degree financially attainable.</p>
<p>There are times, however, when a reduction of grant or other aid may be required in order to remain compliant with federal or other state financial aid regulations.</p>
<p>Our long-standing approach at Cal Poly Pomona is consistent with the newly signed <a href="https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220AB288">Assembly Bill 288</a> (AB 288), which enacts the <strong>California Ban on Scholarship Displacement Act of 2021.<br/></strong></p>
<h3><strong><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Assembly Bill 288 - California Ban on Scholarship Displacement Act of 2021</span></strong></h3>
<p><a href="https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202120220AB288">Assembly Bill 288</a> (AB 288), signed into law on September 30, 2022, enacts the <strong>California Ban on Scholarship Displacement Act of 2021</strong>, which is intended to ensure that <strong><em>private scholarships</em></strong> for students supplement, and do not supplant, gift aid, grants, scholarships, tuition waivers, and fellowship stipends provided by institutions of higher education to California students who have financial need.</p>
<p>AB 288 applies to private institutions of higher (postsecondary) education in California that receive, or benefit from, state-funded financial assistance or enroll students who receive state-funded student financial assistance, as well as students who are California residents who enroll in any public or private institution of higher education to obtain an undergraduate degree.</p>
<p>Commencing with the 2023&#8211;2024 academic year, AB 288 prohibits covered institutions of higher education from reducing the institutional gift aid offer of a student who is eligible to receive a Federal Pell Grant award or financial assistance under the California Dream Act for an academic year as a result of private scholarship awards designated for the student unless the student&#8217;s gift aid exceeds the student&#8217;s annual cost of attendance. A student&#8217;s <strong>institutional gift aid</strong> offer may be reduced, however, by no more than the amount of the student&#8217;s gift aid that is in excess of the student&#8217;s annual cost of attendance.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gift aid</strong> means all financial aid designated for the student&#8217;s educational expenses, including a grant, scholarship, tuition waiver, fellowship stipend, or other third-party payment, that is not a loan or pursuant to a work-study program.</li>
<li><strong>Institutional gift aid</strong> means gift aid that is paid for by the institution of higher education from its funds and the recipient of the aid is selected by the institution.</li>
</ul>
<p>An institution of higher education is further prohibited from considering receipt or anticipated receipt of private scholarships when considering a student who is eligible to receive a Federal Pell Grant award or financial assistance under the California Dream Act for qualification for <strong>institutional gift aid</strong>.</p>
<p>AB 288 encourages institutions of higher education to implement efforts to avoid scholarship displacement through consultation with scholarship providers and students to avoid situations where institutional gift aid and private scholarships can only be used for specific purposes.</p>
<p>The provisions of AB 288 are severable, which means that if any provision of AB 288 or its application is held invalid due to a conflict with federal requirements, the other valid provisions or applications remain in effect.</p>
<h3><strong><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Compliance with Federal and State financial aid rules are still required</span></strong></h3>
<p>Even with the ban on Private Scholarship Displacement, there are times when a reduction of grant or other aid may be required in order to remain compliant with federal or other financial aid regulations, (including provisions surrounding the <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_685_Federal_Direct_Loan_Program#suba685102">treatment of estimated financial assistance</a>):</p>
<ul>
<li>A student&#8217;s Federal Pell Grant <strong><em>will never be reduced</em></strong>, regardless of other aid a student is receiving.</li>
<li>If a student is receiving more than one type of aid that can only be used for specific purposes, such as a private scholarship specifically designated for the Tuition Fee and a State University Grant or Cal Grant Tuition Grant.</li>
<li>If a student is receiving aid that is required to be adjusted based on other aid received (Federal Subsidized Loan, Federal Work Study, California Middle Class Scholarship)</li>
<li>If a student is receiving aid (other than a Federal Pell Grant and Scholarships) that exceeds their Cost of Attendance.</li>
</ul>
<p>At Cal Poly Pomona, we remain in constant consultation with institutional and private scholarship providers to see if scholarship and gift aid can be used for books, room and board or other fees not covered in a student&#8217;s aid package. In those instances when we do need to adjust aid, we will make adjustments in a way that most benefits the student, beginning with reducing any student loans or Work-Study offers &#8211; so that gift aid is maximized and loan debt is kept to a minimum.</p>
<p><strong>AB 288 takes effect on January 1, 2023 &#8211; with the 2023 &#8211; 2024 academic year.</strong></p></p>

                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">September 2022:Is a Scholarship or Fellowship considered Estimated Financial Assistance if it is awarded for a period of nonattendance?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><strong>Answer:</strong> Yes, the award is considered estimated financial assistance (EFA) <strong><em>even if it is received for a period of nonattendance</em></strong>, such as a summer period. The only exception to this rule would be for any non-need-based employment portion of a fellowship (or assistantship or internship).&#160;</p>
<p>The following is guidance from the U.S. Department of Education (ED):</p>
<p>The definition of estimated financial assistance in <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_685_Federal_Direct_Loan_Program#suba685102">34 CFR 685.102(b)</a>, specifically, clause (1)(iii), states: "<em>Estimated financial assistance</em>:</p>
<ul>
<li>The estimated amount of assistance for a period of enrollment that a student (or a parent on behalf of a student) will receive from Federal, State, institutional, or other sources, such as scholarships, grants, net earnings from need-based employment, or loans, including but not limited to- ... (iii) <strong>Any educational benefits paid because of enrollment in a postsecondary education institution, or to cover postsecondary education expenses</strong>;..."</li>
</ul>
<p>The assumption for this part of the definition is that the student would not be receiving the assistance if he or she was not enrolled at a postsecondary institution. [685.102(b)] See also <a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/part_673_campus-based_common_provisions#subb6735">673.5(c) <em>Estimated financial assistance</em></a>.</p>
<p>Under <a href="https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=(title:%20section:1087kk%20edition:prelim)%20OR%20(granuleid:USC-prelim-title-section1087kk)&amp;f=treesort&amp;edition=prelim&amp;num=0&amp;jumpTo=true">Section 471 of the Higher Education Act of 1965 (HEA)</a>, as amended, [20 USC 1087kk], the amount of need for a student for Title IV assistance during a period of enrollment is equal to the cost of attendance (COA) for the student, minus his or her expected family contribution (EFC), and minus his or her estimated financial assistance (EFA).</p>
<p>If an award covers an educational expense that can be included in the student's cost of attendance (COA), any funds paid to the student to cover that expense are considered EFA, regardless of whether funds were paid directly to the student or reimbursed on behalf of the student. Additionally, even if funds are earmarked to cover costs that are not included in the COA (such as a recommended optional enrichment activity or expenses paid outside of the period of enrollment), <strong><em>those funds are still EFA if they are awarded or paid to students because of postsecondary enrollment</em></strong>.</p>
<p>Section 472 of the HEA provides the elements that are allowed in constructing a COA for an enrolled student based only on the costs for the period of enrollment. Since the recipients of the summer scholarships or fellowships are not enrolled in the summer, the COA for the period of enrollment may not include the summer expenses and there is not a separate COA allowed for the summer period of non-enrollment.</p>
Further, per ED, it does not matter whether summer is a header or trailer. If the scholarship, fellowship, assistantship, or internship (or need-based work study) is awarded during a period of nonattendance, <strong>it must be treated as EFA</strong> <strong>in the upcoming award year/academic year</strong>.<br/> <br/> <strong>Note: </strong>The above guidance applies regardless of what the award is called, and it includes such awards as scholarships, fellowships, assistantships, and internships with grant/scholarship and work components, as well as need-based employment programs (Federal Work Study, Learning Aligned Employment Program).</p>

                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="April 2022:Are Graduation Prizes or Post-Enrollment Awards Considered Estimated Financial Assistance (EFA) for the Prior Year?" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">April 2022:Are Graduation Prizes or Post-Enrollment Awards Considered Estimated Financial Assistance (EFA) for the Prior Year?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Scenario:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> A department or college wants to give a monetary graduation prize to students, rather than offering the funds as a scholarship during the academic year.&#160; Do these funds need to be reported to the Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships and included (retroactively) in the student&#8217;s financial aid package as estimated financial aid assistance?. </span><span>&#160;<br/></span><span>&#160;<br/></span><span data-contrast="auto">Answer: Yes, according to guidance from the U.S. Department of Education (ED), the funds would be considered to be estimated financial assistance (EFA) and must be reported to the Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships. </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/part_673_campus-based_common_provisions#subb6735"><span data-contrast="none">34 CFR 673.5(c)</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">&#8239;and </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_685_Federal_Direct_Loan_Program#suba685102"><span data-contrast="none">685.102(b)</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> specify the definition of&#8239;EFA as:</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The estimated amount of assistance for a period of enrollment that a student (or a parent on behalf of a student) will receive from federal, state, institutional, or other sources, such as scholarships, grants, net earnings from need-based employment, or loans, </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">including</span></strong> <strong><span data-contrast="auto">but not limited to</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">, among other things, any educational benefits </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">paid because of enrollment in a postsecondary education institution</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">, or to cover postsecondary education expenses.&#160;&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559685&quot;:720,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Since the money is paid because of postsecondary enrollment it must be included as EFA and Federal and State financial aid adjustments would need to occur to reduce any overaward (receipt of aid that exceeds the student&#8217;s Cost of Attendance &#8211; or financial need)</span><span data-contrast="none">.&#160;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="none">A note of caution from the U.S. Department of Education: </span></strong><span data-contrast="none">Post-enrollment and graduation awards are EFA if they are awarded due to postsecondary enrollment or to pay postsecondary costs (even retroactively).</span> <span data-contrast="none">If the award in question is from the institution itself, or if the institution has any say in how that award is set up, an auditor and program reviewer </span><strong><i><span data-contrast="none">will question whether the institution is</span></i></strong> <strong><i><span data-contrast="none">intentionally delaying</span></i></strong><span data-contrast="none"> the receipt of the award in order to circumvent the intent of the&#8239;law and&#8239;regulation (which has the force of law) related to the treatment of EFA.&#160; This could jeopardize the institution&#8217;s continued participation in the federal student aid programs as well as state aid programs.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="March 2022: Can the Orientation fee be paid with a student's financial aid?" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">March 2022: Can the Orientation fee be paid with a student's financial aid?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Scenario:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> All incoming Undergraduate Students must attend New Student Orientation, which has a fee of $87 that is charged to all participants. Can the Orientation Fee be covered by a student's Fall semester financial aid?</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Answer:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">&#8239;Unfortunately, no.&#160;&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Federal (Title IV) financial aid funds can be applied towards institutional charges under </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subk668164d1"><span>34 CFR 668.164(d)(1)</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">&#8239;</span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">if the charges are</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> current and allowable.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">To be considered an allowable charge, the fee must be charged to a specific block of students and be related to the student's educational pursuit. The orientation </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">activity</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> must take place within the academic year to be considered a current charge.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">While the Orientation Fee </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">is</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> charged to a specific group of students (all incoming Undergraduate students), the Orientation activity takes place during the summer &#8211; which is outside (prior) to the start of the academic year.&#160;</span></p>
<p><span><strong>Additionally</strong></span><span>, under </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subc66832"><span>34 CFR 668.32</span></a><span> a student is considered a </span><span><strong>regular student</strong></span><span> if they have been accepted into an eligible program of study. At Cal Poly Pomona, students are considered accepted for admission </span><span><strong>effective </strong></span><span>with the</span><span><strong> Fall term (or Spring </strong></span><span>&#8211; for Spring admits). As such, the Orientation Activity is taking place prior to when the students meet the definition of a </span><span><strong>regular student</strong></span><span>. The student</span><span>'</span><span>s financial aid cannot be applied to charges for an activity that occurs prior to when they meet the definition of a regular student.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span>As a result, at Cal Poly Pomona the orientation fee cannot be included in the student's Financial Aid Cost of Attendance budget </span><span><strong>nor</strong></span><span> paid with federal or state financial aid</span><span data-contrast="none">.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="February 2022:Are Emergency Grants or Gift Cards from a department required to be coordinated with the Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships in all cases?" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">February 2022:Are Emergency Grants or Gift Cards from a department required to be coordinated with the Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships in all cases?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><span data-contrast="auto">Yes, </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">emergency aid or grant programs</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> administered by the school are considered estimated financial assistance (EFA), and must be coordinated with the financial aid office to ensure a student receiving such assistance is not overawarded with Title IV aid.&#160; This is true whether the emergency aid is from the </span><i><span data-contrast="auto">Broncos Care Basic Needs Program</span></i><span data-contrast="auto"> or is from a specific College or Department wishing to provide emergency grant aid.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The rules governing financial aid are such that </span><strong><i><span data-contrast="auto">any support</span></i></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> that comes from the campus (</span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">including gift cards</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">)</span> <span data-contrast="auto">is considered estimated financial assistance (EFA) and must be incorporated into the financial aid package. This also applies to gift cards or certificates for groceries, Amazon, VISA, etc.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The coordination of resources and COA is required by federal regulations. Additionally, CSU has established policy regarding the required coordination of monetary awards, grants, scholarships and stipends with the student&#8217;s other financial aid:&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<ul>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="1" data-font="Symbol" data-leveltext="&#61623;" data-listid="4"><span data-contrast="auto">The definition of </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">estimated financial assistance</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> is in </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_685_Federal_Direct_Loan_Program#suba685102b"><span data-contrast="none">34 CFR&#8239;685.102(b)</span></a><span data-contrast="none">.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Generally speaking, assistance provided to a student because of his or her enrollment in a postsecondary education program is EFA. Funds from the institution </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">are not excluded</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> from the definition of EFA. Neither are funds awarded on the basis of an emergency experienced by the student. The school </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">must treat the funds as EFA</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="2" data-font="Symbol" data-leveltext="&#61623;" data-listid="4"><span data-contrast="auto">Section 668.16(b)(3) of the administrative capability regulations requires all campus offices to report to the financial aid office any information which may have bearing on a student's receipt of Title IV federal student aid. [34 CFR </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_668_General_Provisions#subb66816"><span data-contrast="none">668.16(b)(3)</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">; </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/part_673_campus-based_common_provisions#subb6735"><span data-contrast="none">673.5</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">; </span><a href="https://www.nasfaa.org/Part_685_Federal_Direct_Loan_Program#subb685201"><span data-contrast="none">685.201</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="3" data-font="Symbol" data-leveltext="&#61623;" data-listid="4"><a href="https://www.calstate.edu/csu-system/administration/codedmemos/Academic%20Affairs%20Coded%20Memos/AA-2004-15.pdf"><strong><span data-contrast="none">CSU Academic Affairs Coded Memo AA-2004-15 (PDF)</span></strong></a><span data-contrast="auto"> issued May 2004, in response to audit findings, reminded campuses of the need to coordinate information and resources related to student financial aid eligibility.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="1" data-font="Symbol" data-leveltext="&#61623;" data-listid="4"><a href="https://www.calstate.edu/csu-system/administration/codedmemos/Academic%20Affairs%20Coded%20Memos/ASA-2016-28.pdf"><strong><span data-contrast="none">CSU Academic Affairs Coded Memo ASA 2016-28 (PDF)</span></strong></a><span data-contrast="auto"> issued November 2016 (and revised December 2021) reiterated that if a student receives awards that would cause their total aid </span><strong><i><span data-contrast="auto">from all sources</span></i></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> to exceed the COA, then adjustments must be made. The Memo specified that, &#8220;in general, unless the award is considered wages, it is other financial assistance and must be factored in.&#8221;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559738&quot;:160,&quot;335559739&quot;:120,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
</ul>
<p aria-level="2"><strong><span data-contrast="none">Reporting grants or gift cards to the Office of Financial Aid &amp; Scholarships HELPS students!</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559738&quot;:40,&quot;335559739&quot;:0,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">In some cases, the institutional emergency grant or gift card is awarded because the student is experiencing an unanticipated expense. Another reason for the financial aid office to be made aware of these situations and student circumstances is that our office can determine whether the use of </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">professional judgment (PJ)</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> would be appropriate.&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></p>
<ul>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="2" data-font="Wingdings" data-leveltext="&#61656;" data-listid="5"><span data-contrast="auto">For example, the Financial Aid staff may determine, based on the circumstances that the student is experiencing that an override to the student&#8217;s dependency status &#8211; or an adjustment to their family&#8217;s income may be warranted.&#160; In these instances &#8211; the student could end up being </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">eligible for additional grants</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> or other need-based aid.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:120,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
<li aria-setsize="-1" data-aria-level="1" data-aria-posinset="3" data-font="Wingdings" data-leveltext="&#61656;" data-listid="5"><span data-contrast="auto">Alternatively, our office might determine that an increase to the student's cost of attendance (COA) is warranted. The COA increase </span><strong><span data-contrast="auto">might offset the EFA</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto">, with the net effect that the student's eligibility for Title IV funds is not impacted. As with all PJ decisions, documentation of the student's circumstances and the rationale of the decision must be maintained.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559738&quot;:120,&quot;335559739&quot;:120,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&#160;</span></li>
</ul></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
                            </section>
                                                        
                                                                                    
                            <section class="accordion" role="tablist">
                                <div class="accordion__item">
                                    <button aria-expanded="false" aria-label="January 2022:How does pursuit of a Double-Major impact an undergraduate students financial aid?" class="accordion__header" tabindex="0">
                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">January 2022:How does pursuit of a Double-Major impact an undergraduate students financial aid?</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>
                                            </svg>
                                        </div>
                                    </button>
                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><p><span data-contrast="auto">Students do not receive additional financial aid eligibility when pursuing more than one major. Additionally, once students meet the degree requirements to complete one of the majors, they are considered to have earned a degree, even if they are still completing the requirements for their other major.&#160; As a result, they are not eligible to receive any further federal or state aid.&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true,&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:240}"> <br/></span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">For example, a student is pursuing a Bachelor&#8217;s of Science degree with majors in Chemical Engineering and Chemistry. At the end of spring semester the student meets the requirements to complete the Chemical Engineering degree, but will not finish the Chemistry major until after the end of the fall semester. This student is not eligible for any federal, state of California, or Cal Poly Pomona Grant aid (including the State University Grant) for the fall semester, because the student is considered to have already earned a first Bachelor&#8217;s degree.&#160; The student could still be eligible for Scholarships (from institutional or external sources)&#160; or for private education loans.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true,&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:240}"><br/></span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Students with more than one major must also adhere to the Cal Poly Pomona </span><a href="https://www.cpp.edu/financial-aid/resources/understanding-sap.shtml"><span data-contrast="none">Satisfactory Academic Progress Standards for Aid Recipients </span></a><span data-contrast="auto">(including the Maximum Time Frame Standard) and are limited by </span><a href="https://studentaid.gov/understand-aid/types/loans/subsidized-unsubsidized"><span data-contrast="none">Aggregate Loan Limits</span></a><span data-contrast="auto"> for Federal Direct Loans.&#160;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true,&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:240}">&#160;</span></p></p>
                                    </div>
                                </div>
