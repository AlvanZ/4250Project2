                                        <p class="body2"><p>Students pursuing an undergraduate degree or teaching credential are potentially eligible to receive a Federal Pell Grant. Students are limited to receiving a Federal Pell Grant for the equivalent of <strong>twelve semesters of full-time enrollment (6 years)</strong>. The calculation of this total includes attendance at Cal Poly Pomona and any other schools where a Pell Grant was paid (including Community Colleges).&#160; <br/><br/>Your Pell Grant usage is tracked by federal student aid according to a percentage called Lifetime Eligibility Used (LEU), and students may not exceed a 600% LEU. <div _ngcontent-lyd-c8="" class="row no-gutters justify-content-between">
<h3 class="fsa-mt-px-8 " id="how-is-my-federal-pell-grant-lifetime-eligibility-used-calculated" tabindex="-1"><span _ngcontent-lyd-c15="" linkhandling="">How is my Federal Pell Grant Lifetime Eligibility Used calculated?</span></h3>
</div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c18="" class="col">
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph"><strong><span _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="d-inline-block">Scheduled award</span></strong>: The maximum amount of Federal Pell Grant funding you can receive is calculated for an aid year. An aid year at Cal Poly Pomona consists of the Fall and Spring terms (i.e., the 2022-2023 aid year consists of Fall 2022 and Spring 2023)</p>
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph"><strong>Your scheduled award</strong></p>
<ul>
<li class="paragraph">is partially determined by using your Expected Family Contribution (EFC) that is calculated from the information you (and your family) provided when you filed your <em>Free Application for Federal Student Aid</em> (FAFSA<sup>&#174;</sup>) form;</li>
<li class="paragraph">is the maximum amount you would be able to receive for the aid year if you were enrolled full-time for the full school year; and</li>
<li class="paragraph">represents 100% of your Pell Grant eligibility for that award year.</li>
</ul>
<em>Review <a href="understanding-enrollment-requirements.shtml">Understanding Enrollment Requirements</a> for details on how your enrollment status impacts the amount of the grant you may receive.</em></div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c18="" class="col"></div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c18="" class="col"><strong>Percent used</strong>: To determine how much of the maximum six years (600%) of Pell Grant you have used each year, the U.S. Department of Education (ED) compares the actual amount you received for the award year with your scheduled award amount for that aid year.<span style="font-weight: 400;"></span></div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c18="" class="col">
<p>Your eligibility will be reduced by each term that you receive payment. The percentage of Pell Grant used each semester is based on your enrollment status:</p>
<ul>
<li>50% for full-time</li>
<li>37.5% for three-quarter-time</li>
<li>25% for half-time</li>
<li>12.5% for less than half-time</li>
</ul>
If you receive the full amount of your scheduled award, you will have used 100%. It&#8217;s possible that you might not receive your entire scheduled award for an award year. There are a number of reasons for this, the most common of which are that you are not enrolled for the full year or that you are not enrolled full-time, or both.</div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c8="" class="row no-gutters justify-content-between">
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c8="" class="row no-gutters justify-content-between">
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph"><strong><em>Examples:</em></strong></p>
<ul>
<li class="paragraph"><strong>Your scheduled award for an aid year is $5,000</strong>.&#160; You only enrolled for one semester, and attended full-time during that semester.&#160; You would have <strong>received only $2,500</strong> - which would be <strong>50% of your scheduled award for the aid year</strong>.</li>
<li class="paragraph"><strong>Your scheduled award for an aid year is $5,000</strong>.&#160; You enrolled for both semesters, but only attended three-quarter time during each semester.&#160; You would have <strong>received a total of $3,750</strong> - which would be <strong>75% of your scheduled award for the aid year</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div _ngcontent-lyd-c8="" class="row no-gutters justify-content-between">
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph">In certain situations, it is possible that you may be eligible to receive up to 150% of your scheduled award for an aid year. For example, if your scheduled award is $5,000 for the aid year and you received $7,500 because you were enrolled full-time in the fall, spring, <strong>and</strong> summer semesters, you would have received 150% of the scheduled award for that aid year.</p>
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph"><strong>Lifetime Eligibility Used (LEU)</strong>: Federal Student Aid keeps track of your LEU by adding together the percentages of your Pell Grant scheduled awards that you received for each aid year. If your LEU equals or exceeds 600%, you may no longer receive Pell Grant funding.</p>
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph">If your LEU is greater than 500% but less than 600%, while you will be eligible for a Pell Grant for the next aid year, you will not be able to receive a full scheduled award.</p>
<h3 class="paragraph">Track your Pell LEU</h3>
<p _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="paragraph">You may <strong>track your Pell LEU</strong> through Studentaid.gov (log in to <a href="https://studentaid.gov/fsa-id/sign-in">&#8220;My Aid&#8221;</a> using your <span _ngcontent-lyd-c16="" class="d-inline-block"><a aria-expanded="false" class="glossary-term" data-glossary-href="/glossary/fsa-id.json" href="javascript:void(0)" id="glossary-fsa-id" tabindex="0">FSA ID</a></span> to view your LEU).</p>
</div>

<h3><strong>Loan Proration for Graduating Undergraduate Students</strong></h3>
<div class="wpb_text_column wpb_content_element ">
<div class="wpb_wrapper">
<p>Loan proration impacts undergraduate students applying for graduation who are receiving Federal Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans.</p>
<p>Federal regulations require schools to prorate the Federal Direct Loan amounts for&#160;<strong>graduating undergraduate students</strong>&#160;when their final period of enrollment is less than a full academic year. The loan limit proration determines the maximum loan amount that a student may borrow for their final term of study.&#160;</p>
<p>Graduating undergraduate students who are&#160;<strong>only attending one semester</strong>&#160;of the academic year will have their Federal Direct Loans prorated based on the number of credit hours they are enrolled in.</p>
<p><em>Note: Teaching Credential and Graduate students are excluded from the loan proration requirement.</em></p>
<h3><strong>Aggregate (Lifetime) Loan Limits</strong></h3>
<div _ngcontent-hrq-c8="" class="row no-gutters justify-content-between">
<p _ngcontent-hrq-c16="" class="paragraph">In addition to the annual limits, there are limits on the total amounts that you may borrow for your specific program of study (Undergraduate, Teaching Credential, and Graduate).&#160; These limits are refered to as the aggregate loan limits.</p>
<div _ngcontent-sob-c19="" class="row no-gutters">
<div _ngcontent-sob-c20="">
<ul>
<li class="row no-gutters">The <strong>credential aggregate</strong> limit includes all federal loans received for undergraduate study.</li>
<li class="row no-gutters">
<p _ngcontent-hrq-c16="" class="paragraph">The <strong>graduate aggregate limit</strong> includes all federal loans received for undergradaute or teaching credential study.</p>
</li>
<li class="row no-gutters">
<p _ngcontent-hrq-c16="" class="paragraph">If the total loan amount you recieve over the course of your education reaches the aggregate loan limit, you are not eligible to receive additional loans.</p>
</li>
<li class="row no-gutters">
<p _ngcontent-hrq-c16="" class="paragraph">If you reach the aggregate limit and repay some of your loans to bring your outstanding loan debt below the aggregate loan limit, you could then borrow again - up to the amount of your remaining eligibility under the aggregate loan limit.</p>
</li>
</ul>
