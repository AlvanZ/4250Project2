            menu.removeClass("hidden");

            });

                                        <p class="body2"><p>SAP is measured after each semester at Cal Poly Pomona (Fall, Spring, Summer) and determines your eligibility to receive aid for the next semester of enrollment.</p>
<h3><strong>Step 1:</strong></h3>
<p>To view your <strong>SAP status</strong>, from the left navigation bar, select <strong>View SAP Status</strong> (under the Financial Section).</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>

</tbody>
</table>
<h3><strong>Step 2:</strong></h3>
<p>This will open your <strong>Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)&#160;for the Receipt of Financial Aid </strong>page.</p>
<p><img alt="image of BroncoDirect SAP Status screen" class="img img-original-size" height="768" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-fullscreen.jpg" width="1000"/></p>
<h3><strong>Understanding the SAP Status Screen</strong></h3>
<p><strong>The SAP</strong> <strong>Status</strong> screen is broken down into specific sections, with an overview of your evaluation appearing at the top.&#160; The overview contains information regarding when the evaluation was done, your academic program the evaluation was based on, and your overall status.</p>
<p>The remainder of the SAP Status screen has an Understanding Your Status section and the Details of SAP Evaluation.</p>
<p><img alt="image of SAP Status screen with the various sections for Understanding Your Status, Details of Evaluation and GPA, Pace, and Maximum Timeframe highlishted" class="img img-original-size" height="768" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-fullscreen_marked.jpg" width="1000"/></p>
<h4><strong>Understanding Your Status:</strong></h4>
<p>This section provides information about your Overall SAP Status and eligibility for financial aid.</p>
<ul>
<li>If your status is Not Meets, information about your eligibility to submit an appeal will be displayed.</li>
<li>If you submitted an appeal and it was approved, information about remaining eligible will be displayed.</li>
<li>To view a complete description of the SAP standards at Cal Poly Pomona, click on the <strong>Cal Poly Pomona SAP Standards for the Receipt of Financial Aid link</strong> within this section. This will take you to the <a href="understanding-sap.shtml">Understanding SAP Page</a> on the Financial Aid website.</li>
</ul>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img alt="image of Understanding your Status section of the SAP Status Screen" class="img img-original-size" height="309" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-understanding-status.jpg" width="1000"/></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h4><br/>Details of SAP Evaluation</h4>
<p>The Details of SAP Evaluation section provides your status under each of the three SAP Standards (either Meet or Fail), as well as details about how you are measuring within each specific standard.</p>
<h4><strong>The GPA Standard:</strong></h4>
<p>A qualitative measure of progress as measured by your cumulative grade point average (GPA). This field displays the minimum GPA required by Cal Poly Pomona and your current cumulative GPA.</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img alt="image of GPA section of the SAP Status page" class="img img-original-size" height="156" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-gpa-standard.jpg" width="1000"/></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h4><strong>The Pace Standard:</strong></h4>
<p>A quantitative measure which calculates the pace at which you are progressing toward program completion.</p>
<p>The required percentage is displayed as is your current percentage of units completed.&#160; You can also see the information about units attempted and units completed that were included in the calculation.</p>
<p><em>You <strong>must satisfactorily complete at least 67%</strong> of the cumulative credit hours attempted through your academic career in order to be able to complete your program within the Maximum Timeframe.&#160; For a detailed description of the types of units that are counted or are excluded for Pace, please refer to the <a href="understanding-sap.shtml">Cal Poly Pomona Understanding SAP</a> page.</em></p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img alt="image of Pace section of the SAP Status page." class="img img-original-size" height="163" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-pace-standard.jpg" width="1000"/></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h4><strong>The Maximum Time Frame Standard:</strong></h4>
<p>A quantitative measure to determine if you are completing the academic program within a reasonable overall time frame based on <em><strong>attempted</strong></em> <strong><em>units</em></strong>. This field displays the maximum attempted units allowed for your academic program and the maximum units you have remaining.</p>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td><img alt="image of the Maximum Timeframe section of SAP Status screen" class="img img-original-size" height="188" src="../img/broncodirect-sap-max-timeframe-standard.jpg" width="1000"/></td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p><strong><em>Please note: </em></strong><em>Remaining Units Attempted Allowed</em> refers to the units remaining for the term in which SAP has been evaluated. Current units in progress are not deducted from the remaining unit total until the next SAP evaluation. <em>For a detailed description of the types of units that are counted or are excluded for Maximum Time Frame, please refer to the <a href="understanding-sap.shtml">Understanding SAP</a> page.</em></p>
For additional information on SAP appeals and procedures please refer to our <a href="understanding-sap.shtml">Understanding SAP</a></p>

                                        <h3 class="h6__proxima">Tips for a Successful Appeal</h3>
                                        <div class="accordion__item--right">
                                            <svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" class="accordion-chevron" fill="none" height="17" viewBox="0 0 29 17" width="29">
                                                <path d="M27 1.5L14.5 14.5L2 1.5" stroke="#005030" stroke-width="3"/>

                                    <div class="accordion__content" tabindex="0">
                                        <p class="body2"><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Take Time to Review Your History<br/></strong></span>
<p>SAP is based on your cumulative / overall academic history - not just your most recent term of attendance.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Review your Unofficial Transcript</strong> to identify the semesters where you failed to complete classes or received low grades.&#160;
<ul>
<li>Which terms / years did you experience obstacles to your success (a grade received, or unable to successfully complete a course)?&#160;</li>
<li>What caused those obstacles?</li>
<li>Create a timeline of events to review with your advisor.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Review your Degree Progress Report</strong> to identify which program requirements have been satisfied and which ones still must be completed. This will help in determining how to best plan your enrollment and development of any academic plans.</li>
</ul>
<p>&#160;</p>
<h3><strong><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Provide specifics</span></strong></h3>
<p>Be as <strong>specific as possible</strong> in your Statement of Appeal.&#160; SAP is a cumulative review of your academic performance; review your enrollment activity for <strong>all terms</strong> you have attended.&#160;</p>
<ul>
<li>What are specific examples of changes that you will make or resources that you will use to ensure those challenges don't occur again - and to ensure your future academic success (e.g., EOP advising, counseling, tutoring, etc.).<br/>
<ul>
<li><strong>Instead of&#160;</strong> <em>I plan to study more, attend all my classes, and try harder</em></li>
<li><strong>State</strong> <em>I am meeting with my professor once a week for chemistry. I have started keeping a planner to mark out assignment deadlines. I will be getting tutoring for my engineering courses. I have reduced my hours at work to 15 hours per week, and will only work on days I don't have class</em>.<br/><br/></li>
<li><strong>Instead of</strong> <em>I will continue to do my best.</em></li>
<li><strong>State</strong> <em>I have an accountability partner that will check with me weekly. I will meet with my academic advisor the first Tuesday of every month. I am meeting with a counselor at from CAPS to talk about my parent&#8217;s divorce.</em></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Failure to adequately explain your strategies for success may result in your appeal being denied.</p>
<h3><strong><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Attach any relevant supporting documentation</span></strong></h3>
<p>Supporting documentation is not required, however based on your situation or circumstances, you may wish to include documentation to further support your appeal and increase the likelihood of approval.</p>
<p>Some examples of supporting documentation include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Doctor's statement on official letterhead</li>
<li>Copy of hospital/urgent care/physician's bill</li>
<li>Obituary, funeral notice or death certificate</li>
<li>Changes to work schedule</li>
<li>Notice of accommodations from Disability Resource Center</li>
</ul>
<p>The documentation you submit will depend on your individual situation (some situations may not have documentation).&#160;</p></p>
