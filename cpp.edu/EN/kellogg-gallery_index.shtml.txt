                                            <img alt="kellogg university art gallery" class="image-right-medium" height="475" src="img/kellogg.png" style="max-width: 350px; height: auto;" width="640"/>
<p>The W. Keith &amp; Janet Kellogg University Art&#160;Gallery was built by the John L. and Helen Kellogg Foundation with support from W. Keith and Janet &#8220;Jean&#8221; Kellogg in 1988.&#160; This gift was in the spirit of W. Keith&#8217;s grandfather, cereal magnate W.K. Kellogg, who donated his land to the State of California for use by Cal Poly in 1949.</p>
<figure class="fig-350 left" style="margin-top: 8px;"><img alt="kellogg art gallery entrance.  navigate down for further details" height="800" src="img/kellogg-art-gallery-entrance.jpg" style="max-width: inherit; height: auto;" width="1600"/>
<figcaption class="env-artwork-descr"><span style="font-size: 10pt;"><em>The Gates</em> by Italian architect and designer, Ettore Sottsass (1917-2007).</span></figcaption>
</figure>
<p>The&#160;Kellogg Gallery is&#160;located in Building 35A on the North&#160;end of the Bronco Student Center and&#160;is part of the College of Environmental Design, which houses the departments of Art, Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Urban and Regional Planning, and the Lyle Center for Regenerative Studies. Our exhibition space is approximately 4,000 sq. ft. with an entry courtyard featuring&#160; <em>The Gates</em>&#160;and&#160; <em>Black Marble Columns with Lintel</em>&#160;by Italian architect and designer, Ettore Sottsass (1917-2007).&#160;</p>
<p>Kellogg Gallery utilizes its close proximity to Los Angeles as a resource in presenting timely and engaging exhibitions of Contemporary Art in a variety of contexts.&#160; In 2011, we presented our first international show, <em>Persian Visions</em>, <span></span></p>
<figure class="fig-350 right"><span style="font-size: 10pt;"><img alt="kellogg gallery exterior.  navigate down for further information" class="image-right-medium" height="800" src="img/kellogg-gallery-exterior.jpg" style="max-width: inherit; height: auto;" width="1600"/></span>
<figcaption class="env-artwork-descr"><span style="font-size: 10pt;"><em>Black Marble Columns with Lintel</em> by Italian architect and designer, Ettore Sottsass (1917-2007).</span></figcaption>
</figure>
<p><span></span> a traveling exhibition of recent Iranian photography.&#160;</p>
<p>In the last few years (2018-19), Gallery Curator Michele Cairella Fillmore&#160;featured solo exhibitions of:&#160;tech-based, video-installation art&#160;and photographer&#160;Sasha vom Dorp from Taos, New Mexico;&#160;kinetic-engineering-based Korean&#160;artist David Jang; and a sweeping&#160;40-year&#160;retrospective of Jim Morphesis' painting, drawings and assemblage.&#160;<em>Somewhere in Between</em>, co-curated with guest artist-curator Bia Gayotto, opened&#160;on Election Day during the 2018 midterms, and showcased&#160;twenty-four LA-based artists of various international origins,&#160;ethnicities and cultures.&#160;These&#160;artists&#160;addressed relevant&#160;concepts of transnationalism, cultural&#160;identity, appropriation, hybridization,&#160;and a sense of place and history.&#160;</p>
<p>Annually exhibited since 1971,&#160; <em>Ink &amp; Clay</em>&#160;has become&#160;a widely recognized, national, juried exhibition held&#160;with support from the James H. Jones Estate and the Office of Cal Poly Pomona President, Dr. Soraya M. Coley. We also proudly present&#160;triennial exhibitions of work by faculty, and&#160;annual exhibitions of&#160;student work from the Department of Art.</p>
